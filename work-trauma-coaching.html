The problem, named directly

It was not "just a job."

Maybe it was a boss who made you doubt yourself. Maybe it was a workplace culture that quietly wore you down. Maybe it was a layoff that shook more than your income; it challenged who you thought you were. Maybe it was something more acute, an experience you would call trauma, even if you have never said that word out loud at work. Whatever shape it took, it is real, and it is still affecting you.

This is space to process what happened, understand its effect on you now, and find your footing again, without minimizing what you went through or needing to have language for all of it yet.

What this support looks like

Work through the aftermath at a pace that makes sense.

This may include processing what happened, identifying patterns it left behind, rebuilding professional confidence after a layoff or identity loss, and restoring a sense of trust in a workplace, in your own judgment, or both. Session format and cadence vary by client.

An important boundary

Coaching, not clinical treatment.

This is coaching, not therapy or clinical trauma treatment. If what you are carrying feels acute or clinical, we will be honest about that and can discuss seeking additional support alongside this work.

Work Trauma Coaching FAQ

Clear answers before you begin.

Is this therapy?

No. We do not diagnose or treat mental-health conditions. This is coaching focused specifically on difficult workplace experiences and their aftereffects.

What if I am still in the workplace where this happened?

That is common, and we can work within that reality rather than assuming you are free to leave immediately.

You do not have to explain it perfectly to begin.

Start with a conversation about what happened, what still feels heavy, and what kind of support may help you move forward.
