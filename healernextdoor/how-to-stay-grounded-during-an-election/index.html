How to Stay Grounded During an Election

healingNovember 3, 2024By Lisa Batitto

As the election approaches, feeling a bit stressed and uncertain is normal. There's overwhelming news and opinions coming from all directions. But don't worry! Here are some simple ways to help you take care of yourself while staying engaged and informed.

Acknowledge What You're Feeling

First things first, it's really important to recognize how you're feeling—whether it's anxiety, anger, or even a sense of hopelessness. Giving a name to your emotions is a big step in managing them. Remember, you don't have to be calm all the time. Just saying, "This is how I feel right now, and that's totally okay," can help you feel more grounded.

Ground Yourself with Mindful Breathing

Whenever you're feeling anxious or overwhelmed, take a moment to pause and focus on your breath. Here's a simple exercise to try:  

1. Inhale deeply for a count of four.  

2. Hold that breath for another count of four.  

3. Exhale slowly for four counts.  

This mindful breathing helps activate your body's calming system, making it easier to feel relaxed and centered. The best part? You can do it anywhere, whether you're cozy at home or on the go. Take a moment for yourself; you deserve it!

Set Boundaries with News and Social Media

You don't have to be glued to your screens to stay informed. It's definitely good to know what's going on, but you can avoid the endless doomscrolling. Try setting specific times to check the news or social media. This way, you can stay in the loop without feeling overwhelmed.

Connect with Others

Just a little reminder that you're not alone in how you're feeling. Chatting with someone you trust about what's on your mind can be really comforting. Whether it's a friend, a family member, or even an online community that vibes with you, opening up in a supportive space can bring you a sense of relief and connection.

Move Your Body to Release Tension

Getting a little bit of physical activity can really help you shake off stress!. It doesn't have to be anything intense—just a short walk, some light stretching, or even shaking out your limbs can do wonders. Even a few minutes of movement can lift your spirits and release that tension.

Channel Your Energy into Action

Sometimes, anxiety can creep in when we feel like we have no control. A great way to take charge is to channel that energy into action! You might find it helpful to volunteer for a cause that inspires you or encourage your friends and family to vote. Even small steps can really help you feel more purposeful. Remember, every little action counts.

Stay Present with What You Can Control

It's easy to get caught up in all the "what ifs" during election season! A great way to stay grounded is to focus on what you can actually control. Think about things like casting your vote, helping a friend get to the polls, or deciding how to spend your day. By zeroing in on these little actions, you'll remind yourself that you can make a difference, even when things feel uncertain. Enjoy the process!

It's important to stay in the loop with what's happening around us, but don't forget to take care of your mental and emotional health too! It's all about finding that sweet spot where you can stay engaged without feeling overwhelmed. Remember, you deserve that balance, no matter what's happening in the world!
