How We Undermine Our Intuition (And How to Stop)

journeyJuly 8, 2025By Lisa Batitto

Ralph Waldo Emerson, the American philosopher, once stated, “The primary wisdom is intuition.” This concept is an important part of our human experience. While we may occasionally tap into our intuition, doing so consistently can lead to significant insights and outcomes. So, why don’t we make the most of this gift more often?

We often unknowingly sabotage our natural intuition. Let’s explore common pitfalls and discover how we can overcome them.

We Rush Through LifeI
n our hustle, we forget to pause and listen. Consider taking a moment to slow down or simply be still. Your intuition awaits in the quiet.

We Ignore Symbolism
Life is full of messages and signs, but we often overlook them. Stay open to the deeper meanings and possibilities that surround you.

We Let Our Ego Take Charge
Our ego craves control, which can overshadow our intuition. Recognize when your ego is holding you back and welcome your intuitive voice instead.

We Confuse Intuition with Fear
It’s easy to mix up intuitive nudges with anxiety or wishful thinking. By stepping outside of our emotional whirlwinds, we can reconnect with the true wisdom of our intuition.

We Surround Ourselves with Doubters
Associating with those who dismiss intuition can dampen our instincts. Remember, intuition is contagious, so nurture relationships with like-minded people who value it.

We Try to Force It
Unlike pedaling a bike or lifting weights, intuition cannot be summoned on demand. Instead, invite it in gently and allow it to emerge naturally.

We Rely Solely on Logic
Sticking rigidly to analytical thought can stifle our creative problem-solving potential. Embrace emotional intelligence. Combining it with logic can unlock intuitive insights.

We Dismiss Intuitive Signals
Listening to our ego when it claims it can handle everything means risking valuable insights from intuition. Trust that these insights can guide you more effectively.

We Underestimate the Learning Process
Mastering intuition requires tools, skills, and practice. Don’t let yourself be trapped in a cycle of overthinking. Give your intuition the chance to flourish.

Even Albert Einstein recognized the importance of intuition, saying, “The real valuable thing is intuition.” If a mind as analytical as his can appreciate the power of intuition, maybe it’s time for us to explore this remarkable aspect of ourselves. With practice, guidance, and a few successes under our belt, we might uncover the wisdom that intuition has to offer. So, why not give it a shot? You never know what revelations await!
