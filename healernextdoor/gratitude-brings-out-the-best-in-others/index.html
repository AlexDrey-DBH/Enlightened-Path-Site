Gratitude Brings Out the Best in Others

gratitudeAugust 11, 2021By Lisa Batitto

A good relationship starts with you.

It might sound too simple to be true, but expressing positivity and happiness with your relationship will most likely make your partner rise up to match. After all, people see themselves reflected in you and your reactions to them. If you show them how happy they make you, you will tap into and draw out the part of them that makes you smile.

Gratitude is a skill that needs to be learned and honed. As years go by, life's painful experiences accumulate, leaving us feeling burdened and heartbroken. It's not uncommon for you to feel increasingly isolated, even if you are in a relationship.

This can make expressing gratitude to the people we love difficult. We're too busy, distracted, or otherwise mentally consumed, and the person who means the most to us isn’t being acknowledged.

But, if you want your life and your relationship to improve, you need to commit to making small incremental changes that can lead you closer to living a more satisfying life. And the most important lesson is that of giving thanks.

There are many benefits of gratitude, one being that it can have an enormous impact on your relationship. If you are single, you are more likely to find love if you consciously focus on what you have in your life. Remember, you attract more of what you focus on!

Practicing gratitude daily can help you to positively rewire your brain. Changes in the way you think will eventually lead to changes in how you feel and the things you express. And people willna naturally pick up on that positive vibe you are sending out.
