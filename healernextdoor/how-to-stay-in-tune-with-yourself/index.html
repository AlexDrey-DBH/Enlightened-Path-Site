How to Stay in Tune with Yourself

journeyMay 20, 2024By Lisa Batitto

Life moves fast. Between work, family, and endless to-do lists, it's easy to lose track of how you're really doing. Taking a moment to check in with yourself can feel like a luxury, but it's essential for keeping your mental and emotional balance. Here's a fun, easy guide to making self-check-ins a regular part of your routine.

Why Self-Check-Ins Matter

Think of self-check-ins as quick pit stops for your mind and body. Just like a car needs fuel and oil checks, you need to see how you're doing to keep running smoothly. Here are some benefits:

Boost Self-Awareness: Regularly tuning in helps you understand what makes you tick and what ticks you off.

Manage Emotions Better: Recognizing your feelings helps you handle them before they handle you.

Make Better Decisions: When you know your state of mind, you can make choices that truly suit you.

Lower Stress: Pausing to check in can interrupt the stress cycle, a continuous loop of stressors and reactions, and give you a breather. By taking a moment to assess your stress levels and identify the causes, you can break this cycle and take steps to manage your stress more effectively.

How to Do a Self-Check-In

You only need a little time to check in with yourself. Here's a quick and easy way to do it:

Find Your Spot: Choose a comfy, quiet place where you won't be disturbed. Your favorite chair, a cozy corner, or even your car works.

Take a Breather: Close your eyes and take a few deep breaths. Inhale through your nose, hold for a second, then exhale through your mouth. This helps you focus and brings you into the present moment, a key aspect of mindfulness, which is the foundation of self-check-ins.

Body Scan: Mentally scan your body from head to toe. This involves paying attention to any physical sensations or discomfort you might be experiencing. Notice any tight spots or discomfort. Are your shoulders tense? Is your jaw clenched? This step is crucial as it helps you connect with your body and understand how it's responding to your current state of mind.

Name That Emotion: What are you feeling right now? Happy, anxious, tired? Whatever it is, just acknowledge it without trying to change it.

Check Your Thoughts: What's going through your mind? Are your thoughts racing, or are they calm? Are they positive or stressful?

Take Action: Decide if there's something you need to do for yourself. For example, if you notice tension in your shoulders, you might decide to take a break and stretch. If you're feeling hungry, you might decide to have a snack. These actions can help you address your immediate needs and improve your overall well-being.

Making It a Habit: Start by setting specific times for your check-ins. It could be first thing in the morning, during your lunch break, or before bed. The key is to make it a regular part of your day. Remember, it's easier than you think!

Self-check-ins are like mini-recharges for your brain and body. They help you recognize your needs, manage your emotions, and make better choices. By taking a few moments each day to see how you're doing, you'll find yourself more balanced and ready to handle whatever comes your way. So go ahead, give it a try, and make self-check-ins a fun and essential part of your wellness routine!
