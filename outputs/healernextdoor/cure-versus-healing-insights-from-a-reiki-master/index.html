Curing versus Healing: Insights from a Reiki Master

healingApril 29, 2024By Lisa Batitto

How do we know if we need a cure or healing when faced with health challenges? The terms are often spoken of as if they are the same, yet they serve distinct purposes in our journey towards wellness. This understanding invites us to consider the immediate alleviation of symptoms and the broader scope of our health and happiness.

Understanding Cure

Cure is typically associated with the elimination of symptoms or the complete eradication of a disease. It is often seen in the medical context where treatments are directed towards specific illnesses, using medications, surgery, or other medical interventions to address physical ailments. The primary goal is to resolve health issues quickly, usually focusing on the physical body. Cures are measurable and observable, making them a fundamental aspect of conventional medicine.

The Nature of Healing

Healing, on the other hand, is a more holistic approach. It encompasses not just the physical body but also someone's emotional, spiritual, and mental aspects. As a Reiki healer, I view healing as a process—a journey that an individual embarks on to achieve a state of balance and harmony within themselves and with the world around them. It's about treating symptoms and nurturing the whole person to foster resilience and well-being.

Reiki, a form of energy healing, exemplifies this holistic approach. By channeling universal energy to the individual, Reiki works on various levels to restore and balance the life force energy within the body. This doesn't necessarily mean that physical symptoms are immediately resolved. Instead, it aids the body's natural healing processes, often resulting in a deeper sense of peace, reduced stress, and improved emotional equilibrium. Healing through Reiki can lead to profound transformations that might not be visible as a 'cure' but are felt deeply by those who experience them.

Cure vs. Healing in Practice

In practical terms, a cure might be what you seek when battling a bacterial infection—antibiotics are administered to kill the bacteria, aiming for a quick and effective resolution of the infection. In this context, healing might involve addressing the infection and exploring its underlying causes, such as a weakened immune system or stress-related vulnerabilities. Through practices like Reiki, meditation, or dietary changes, the focus would be on strengthening the body's natural defenses and promoting overall health, thus addressing the symptoms and their deeper origins.

Integrating Both Approaches

Embracing both curing and healing can provide the most comprehensive approach to health. While addressing acute and specific medical issues directly and effectively with a cure is important, integrating healing practices like Reiki ensures that all aspects of a person's well-being are nurtured. This integration supports recovery, maintenance of health, and prevention of future ailments.

The Reiki Perspective

From a Reiki perspective, true wellness is achieved when there is harmony between the physical, emotional, mental, and spiritual elements of an individual. Healing is about creating a space for this balance to unfold, empowering individuals to reach an optimal state of health over time. While Reiki can be a powerful tool for healing, it is most effective when used in conjunction with traditional medical treatments, respecting the unique roles that both curing and healing play in our lives.

As we navigate the complexities of health and well-being, understanding the distinctions between curing and healing can guide us towards more informed choices about our health care. Recognizing that each has its place, we can draw upon a full spectrum of resources to support our journey toward a healthier, more balanced life. As we blend the science of curing with the art of healing, we pave the way for a more holistic approach to living well.
